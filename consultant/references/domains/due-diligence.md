# Due Diligence

Consulting-specific DD judgment. Claude already knows what a diligence covers (commercial, operational, financial, and strategic modules and their standard metrics). This file adds the scoping discipline, thresholds, and red-flag patterns that separate a verdict from a data dump.

## Depth Follows the Thesis

Rank the DD types H/M/L by deal-thesis relevance before requesting data. A capability acquisition needs deep operational and talent work, a market consolidation needs deep commercial work. Spread effort evenly and you will be shallow everywhere.

Scope to the 3-5 questions that, once answered, decide the deal. Sequence to kill early: test the thesis-breaking hypothesis in week one, not week four. A cheap early no is the second-best outcome of a diligence.

## Where Findings Actually Come From

- **Reconciliation gaps.** The finding is usually in what fails to reconcile: CIM vs management presentation vs raw data room extracts. Numbers that agree everywhere were prepared, numbers that diverge were discovered.
- **Level triangulation.** Ask the same question at three organizational levels. Convergent answers are probably true. Divergence maps where management's story ends and operations begin.
- **The unprepared cut.** Request one analysis the target did not pre-build (a cohort cut, a customer-level margin ranking). The speed and quality of the response measure both data infrastructure and candor.

## Thresholds

- Customer concentration: top-3 above 30% of revenue is a pricing-of-risk issue, above 40% is a walk trigger. The business is one relationship away from a material miss.
- Cash conversion (OCF / EBITDA) should exceed 80%. Persistent divergence signals aggressive recognition, working-capital consumption, or hidden capex.
- Quality of earnings: build the reported-to-adjusted bridge (non-recurring items, owner benefits, one-time costs, recognition timing). The adjusted number is what the business actually earns, everything else is presentation.
- Working capital: determine whether the business generates or consumes cash as it grows, normalized for seasonality. This sets the cash need at close, and it is routinely missed because the point-in-time snapshot looks fine.

## Red Flags: Escalate, Don't Document

- Revenue concentrated in accounts with no contractual protection
- Key-person dependencies with no succession plan and no retention mechanism
- Material litigation undisclosed in initial representations
- Technology that cannot scale without full replacement
- Financial results that do not reconcile across different reporting views

## Risk Synthesis and Verdict

Tier every finding: critical (deal-killers: resolve or walk), major (deal-adjusters: price into valuation, structure, or terms), minor (integration items: post-close list). Each risk gets likelihood, impact, mitigation, and an owner.

The recommendation is a governing thought in one sentence (proceed / proceed with conditions / do not proceed), strengths and concerns each tied to evidence, valuation adjustments quantified per finding, and conditions precedent listed as gates, not suggestions. Evidence chain throughout: data → finding → implication → recommendation.
