# Change Management

Consulting-specific change judgment. Claude already knows the adoption frameworks (ADKAR-style stage models, Kotter's phases, communication cadences, standard adoption metrics). This file adds only what those primers get wrong or miss.

## Sponsorship Is Measured in Calendar Time

Change dies when sponsorship is delegated. The test is not the kickoff speech but the sponsor's calendar: recurring personal time on the initiative, visible role modeling, and willingness to spend political capital on blockers. If the sponsor will not give calendar minutes, downgrade the initiative's success probability regardless of program quality. See the Influence Model in [firms/mckinsey/process.md](../firms/mckinsey/process.md) for the four-condition version of this test.

## Resistance Is Information

Classify by root cause, because the response differs: logical resistance is answered with evidence (and if the concern is valid, change the plan), psychological with safety nets, sociological with opinion leaders and new norms, political with private engagement and win-win structures. Political resistance is the only type escalation solves.

The dangerous resistance is silent. Vocal resisters can be engaged, silent ones comply on the surface and undermine in practice. Monitor adoption behavior, never stated support.

## Change Load Is Counted at the Receiver

Organizations track initiatives, not the load on a single frontline role. Before adding a change, map every concurrent change already hitting the same population. Saturation shows up as compliance theater: training completed, dashboards green, behavior unchanged. If the receiving population is saturated, sequencing is the intervention, not more communication.

## The Two-Quarter Thesis Test

Track leading indicators (training, manager readiness, early adoption) weekly during deployment and lagging outcomes quarterly. If leading indicators are strong but lagging outcomes stay flat for two quarters, the change is being adopted and the underlying thesis was wrong. That is a strategy finding, not a change-management failure, and it should trigger re-diagnosis, not another communication wave.
