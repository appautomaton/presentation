# Pricing Strategy

Consulting-specific pricing judgment. Claude already knows the textbook layer (EVE mechanics, Van Westendorp and conjoint, Good/Better/Best design, elasticity drivers, decoy theory). This file adds the diagnostics, thresholds, and traps that separate a pricing engagement from a pricing chapter.

## Why Pricing Leads the Lever List

A 1% improvement in realized price typically moves operating profit 8-12% at a typical cost structure, several times the impact of a 1% volume or cost improvement. Open the engagement by running this arithmetic on the client's own P&L: it sizes the prize and buys permission for the harder governance work below.

## Diagnostics Before Design

Run these three before any WTP research or architecture work. They locate where value leaks, and they usually fund the engagement.

**Price-volume-mix (PVM) decomposition.** Isolate how much of the margin change is price vs volume vs mix. The split routes the engagement: price erosion is a pricing problem, volume loss is a commercial problem, mix shift is a portfolio problem. Prescribing pricing for a mix problem is the most common mis-scoping.

**Pocket margin waterfall.** List price → contractual discounts → off-invoice rebates → freight and terms → pocket price. The list-to-pocket gap is leakage, invisible in the P&L because no single line item carries it. Then plot pocket price against account size: a wide band with weak correlation means discounts follow relationships, not value or volume. That scatter is the single most persuasive exhibit in a pricing deck.

**Discount governance audit.** Map who approves discounts, at what thresholds, with what override rate. An override rate above 30% means governance exists on paper only. Fix governance before architecture: new tiers priced into a broken approval chain leak exactly like the old ones. Audit the sales compensation plan in the same pass: if reps are paid on bookings rather than margin, the discount curve is the comp plan speaking, and no architecture survives it.

**Sequencing.** Leakage enforcement pays back in 0-3 months, architecture in 3-9, full realization in 9-18. Structure the engagement so the visible enforcement wins fund the redesign.

## Value Capture Judgment

- EVE's failure mode is inflated differentiation value from soft benefits. Rule: count only differentiation the customer would concede in a live negotiation. If you would not defend the dollar figure across the table, it does not belong in the model.
- Value-sharing calibration: capture 20-40% of value created under weak differentiation, 40-60% moderate, 60-80% strong with high switching costs. The uncaptured remainder is the customer's incentive to switch and stay.
- WTP evidence hierarchy: transaction data > won/lost deal analysis > conjoint > direct survey. Stated willingness to pay consistently overstates revealed willingness. Never set a price from a survey alone.

## Architecture Judgment

- If more than 40% of customers land in the entry tier, the tier boundary is wrong. Move a fence or cut a feature, do not discount the mid tier.
- The mid tier should carry the highest gross margin (highest volume, lowest support intensity). If it does not, the design is subsidizing the edges.
- Usage-based pricing fails on bill shock, not on price level. Default to hybrid: base platform fee for predictability plus usage overage for upside capture.
- Decoy logic works on consumers and prosumers. Sophisticated B2B buying committees see through it, and a transparently weak mid tier costs credibility in procurement.

## Price Changes

- Build the value case before announcing. Frame against value delivered since the last change, never against your costs.
- The asymmetry that governs rollout: a churned account is a permanent loss, a delayed increase is a linear one. Sequence increases segment by segment, protecting high-value at-risk accounts with tailored transitions.
- Grandfather selectively and always with a sunset date. Open-ended legacy pricing becomes a shadow tier that outlives its rationale.
- Watch churn for 90 days after. If churn cost exceeds the revenue gain, the increase was too aggressive, and reversing it publicly is worse than phasing it correctly the first time.

## When Pricing Is Not the Answer

Kill conditions, consistent with the pricing engagement archetype: WTP headroom under 5% in core segments (no pricing action), elasticity beyond −2.0 (volume loss exceeds price gain), an active price war (defend, do not optimize), or price as the top NPS detractor (fix value delivery first).
