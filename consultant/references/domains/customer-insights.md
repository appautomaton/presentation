# Customer Insights

Consulting-specific judgment. Claude already knows the method layer (segmentation criteria, journey mapping, JTBD interview protocols, CLV math, win/loss templates). This file adds the tests and traps that make customer analysis decision-grade.

## Segmentation

The only test that matters: does the segmentation change resource allocation? If two segments receive the same strategy, price, and coverage, merge them. A segmentation nobody staffs differently is a chart, not a strategy.

The strategy-execution trap: needs-based segmentation wins the argument, firmographic segmentation is what sales can execute. The deliverable must map needs segments onto observable proxies (size, industry, tech stack, buying behavior) or the segmentation dies in the field within a quarter.

## Churn: Timing Is the Diagnosis

Never present a blended churn rate. Cohort it, because root cause differs by when churn happens: early churn (first 90 days) indicts onboarding or the sale itself (wrong buyer), mid-life churn indicts unmet needs, competitive displacement, or a departed champion, renewal churn is a deliberate ROI verdict, and involuntary churn is billing operations. Each has a different owner, so a blended number assigns the problem to no one.

Leading indicators beat exit interviews: usage decline, unresolved escalations, and champion role changes predict churn 30-90 days out, while churned customers rationalize. Intervene on the signal, not the survey.

## Jobs and Whitespace

Rank jobs by importance versus satisfaction. Underserved (high importance, low satisfaction) is the opportunity map. Overserved (low importance, high satisfaction) is the simplification and cost-down map, and it is politically harder because someone inside the client built those features. Listen for the job, not the feature: when a customer describes a feature, ask what they were trying to accomplish with it.

## Win/Loss

Interview within 30 days of the decision. After that, buyers rationalize and the data is fiction. Separate "lost to competitor" from "lost to no decision": no-decision losses are often the largest bucket, and they indict the value proposition and urgency case, not sales execution. Present as a win-rate matrix by competitor and segment with the top three drivers of each outcome.

## Moments of Truth

Three to five interactions shape perception disproportionately (onboarding, first escalation, renewal). Invest there rather than smoothing the average journey: a botched escalation destroys more goodwill than ten ordinary touchpoints build.

## CLV

Prefer cohort-based CLV (actual retention curves extrapolated to steady state) over formula CLV: an "average lifespan" input is fiction in any business with mix shift or expansion revenue. For LTV:CAC and payback benchmarks, use web search (see [kpi-reference.md](kpi-reference.md) for preferred sources).
