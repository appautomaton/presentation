# M&A Strategy

Consulting-specific deal judgment. Claude already knows the textbook layer (screening funnels, valuation methods, Day 1 checklists, 100-day plan templates, IMO governance). This file adds the thesis discipline, synergy math, and integration heuristics that determine whether a deal creates value or donates it.

## Deal Thesis

Every acquisition needs a one-paragraph thesis answering three questions: Why this target? Why now? Why us? If the answer to "why us" is no better than a private equity buyer's, you are in an auction you should lose.

| Archetype | Logic | Key risk | Lead diligence question |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scale | Combine to lower unit cost, raise pricing power | Culture and ops dis-synergies | Are cost synergies real and net? |
| Scope | Add adjacent capability, product, or market | Integration destroys the adjacency | Does the adjacency survive integration? |
| Capability | Buy talent, tech, or IP that cannot be built in time | Key people leave post-close | Who exactly must stay, and will they? |
| Consolidation | Roll up a fragmented market | Overpayment in competitive auction | What is the walk price? |
| Transformational | Business-model shift | Execution overwhelms the org | Can leadership run both models at once? |

The thesis determines what matters in diligence. Diligence that does not test the thesis is wasted effort.

## Buy vs Build vs Partner

Decision order, not a scorecard:

1. Can we build it inside the required window with talent we have or can hire within 12 months? If yes, build: it is core and time permits.
2. If not: is ongoing access sufficient, or do we need ownership and control? Access suffices for non-core or fast-evolving capabilities (partner). Ownership needs point to acquire.
3. Preemption test: would a competitor owning the target damage us? If yes, urgency rises and the calculus tilts to acquire, but a defensive premium has a ceiling. Name it before the process starts.

## Synergy Buildup

- The premium buys only the synergies. The market already priced the standalone business, so if net synergies (after haircuts and costs-to-achieve) fall below the premium, the difference is a gift to the seller's shareholders.
- Haircut identified synergies to base rates: 60-80% realization for cost, 40-60% for revenue. Present ranges, not points, and name the assumptions that move the range.
- Model the ramp: cost synergies land in 12-24 months, revenue synergies in 24-36. Year-one savings are offset by one-time costs (restructuring, systems, retention packages). Net them out or the bridge lies.
- Dis-synergies are systematically omitted and always exist: customer attrition where bases overlap, key-person flight, distracted management. A synergy model with a zero dis-synergy line is a red flag in itself.

## Integration Mode

Choose by two factors: strategic interdependence (how much the businesses must share to create the value) and autonomy value (how much the target's culture and operating model are worth preserving).

| Mode | When | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Absorption | High interdependence, low autonomy value | Talent flight, culture destroyed |
| Preservation | Low interdependence, high autonomy value | Synergies stay financial only |
| Symbiosis | Both high | Complexity: function-by-function choices |

Default to symbiosis when unsure. It forces explicit per-function decisions, where absorption and preservation fail precisely by ignoring the dimension they de-prioritize.

Integration planning starts during diligence, not after close, and the tracking metric follows the thesis: a capability deal is measured on talent retention, a scale deal on cost capture rate. A deal that hits its financial targets but loses the people or customers it was built on has failed.

## Walk-Away Discipline

Set the walk price in writing before the auction begins, while nobody is in love with the target. Competitive processes exist to move that number, and every mechanism in them (deadlines, exclusivity, "another interested party") is designed to do so. The highest-value slide in many M&A engagements is the one that recommends not doing the deal.

## Valuation Cross-Check

Use at least two methods (DCF, comps, precedent transactions, SOTP, LBO), explain any divergence, and present a range, not a number. See [financial-analysis.md](financial-analysis.md) for discount rate calibration and decision rules.
