# Engagement Archetypes

Eight recurring engagement types with pillar architectures, kill conditions, and governing thought templates. Load after ROUTE classifies the engagement. Cross-references point to existing firm/domain files for analytical depth.

**Composing archetypes.** Real engagements often span two archetypes. When they do: one leads (governs the GT and pillar structure), the other supports (contributes analysis to specific pillars). The lead archetype is whichever answers the primary decision. Kill conditions from both archetypes apply — a supporting archetype's kill condition can terminate the lead engagement.

---

### Market Entry

**GT+:** "[Company] should enter [market] via [mode: acquisition/JV/organic] targeting [segment] to capture [SAM: $XB] at [X% CAGR], achieving [financial target] by [year], because [external rationale] and [internal rationale]."
**GT−:** "[Company] should not enter [market] because [dealbreaker]. [Alternative] offers better risk-adjusted returns."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Market Opportunity | SAM exceeds threshold with CAGR >X% and no structural barriers |
| 2 | Entry Mode | One mode dominates on speed, cost, and risk-adjusted return |
| 3 | Target & Segment Selection | Priority segment identifiable with targets meeting criteria |
| 4 | Financial Case & Risks | NPV positive base case, risks mitigable, downside survivable |

**Key devices:** TAM→SAM→SOM waterfall, entry mode eval grid, scenario modeling (→ frameworks.md market sizing)
**Kill conditions:** SAM <$XB minimum threshold → don't enter | Regulatory barrier >18mo to clear → defer | All entry modes fail risk screen → partnership only | Incumbent network effects >60% share → differentiate or don't enter
**Quality gates:** SAM (not TAM) in GT; ≥3 entry modes evaluated; downside scenario modeled; peer set for benchmarking named

### Profitability / Cost Transformation

**GT+:** "[Company] can improve EBITDA margin by [X]bps through [N] cost levers across [scope], capturing $[Y]M within [timeframe], because [root cause] creates addressable structural opportunity."
**GT−:** "[Company]'s margin gap is [X]bps, but only [Y]bps addressable without [barrier]. Priority is [alternative]."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Benchmark the Gap | Peer set is valid, gap is real after normalization |
| 2 | Root Cause Analysis | Causes classified as structural vs addressable |
| 3 | Size the Prize | Addressable opportunity exceeds minimum threshold |
| 4 | Design Initiatives | Each initiative has owner, investment, timeline, confidence |
| 5 | Prove the P&L Math | Downside scenario still clears target margin |

**Key devices:** Value Driver Tree (→ McKinsey process.md Tier 2), Waterfall Bridge (3-4× per deck), Peer Benchmarking
**Kill conditions:** Gap <200bps after normalization → narrow to cost optimization only | >60% of gap in non-addressable categories → revenue growth instead | Headcount >15% of workforce → restructuring engagement | No valid peer set → first-principles modeling
**Quality gates:** Peer benchmark n≥5; opportunity presented as ranges, not points; P&L bridge sums correctly; waterfall bridges internally consistent

### Growth Strategy

**GT+:** "[Company] can achieve [X]% revenue growth ($[Y]M) over [timeframe] by focusing on [N] vectors, funded by reallocating $[Z]M from low-momentum segments."
**GT−:** "[Company]'s growth engines are structurally decelerating. Without portfolio shift, organic revenue will decline [X]%. Priority is [harvest/restructure/divest]."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Growth Diagnostic | Historical growth decomposed; share gain vs market tailwind separated |
| 2 | Growth Vector ID | Vectors sized, screened, and ranked by risk-adjusted return |
| 3 | Resource Reallocation | Funding source named, not implied; trade-offs explicit |
| 4 | Growth Roadmap | Sequenced with milestones and decision gates |

**Key devices:** Granular Growth Decomposition (→ McKinsey process.md Tier 2): Portfolio Momentum + M&A + Share Gain; Ansoff Matrix × Three Horizons for vector screening
**Kill conditions:** All vectors below hurdle rate → harvest/divest | Core market declining >5% CAGR → defensive repositioning | Resource reallocation blocked politically → external capital or M&A-led growth
**Quality gates:** GGD reveals actual share performance (not masked by market growth); funding source explicit; ~25% of growth engagements conclude "don't pursue"

### M&A / Due Diligence

**GT+:** "[Company] should acquire [Target] at $[X]M ([Y]× [basis]) because [deal thesis], yielding [return metric] with integration risk focused on [area]."
**GT−:** "[Company] should not acquire at [price] because [dealbreaker]. [Alternative] offers better risk-adjusted returns."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Deal Thesis | Strategic rationale stands independent of synergies |
| 2 | Commercial DD | Revenue is real, sustainable, growing; concentration acceptable |
| 3 | Synergy Quantification | Net synergies (after haircuts and dis-synergies) exceed premium |
| 4 | Valuation & Structuring | ≥3 methods converge within 30% spread |
| 5 | Integration Planning | 100-day plan has specific actions with owners |

**Key devices:** Buy vs Build vs Partner decision tree (→ m-and-a.md), Conditional hypothesis (Bain-style: "acquire IF C1, C2, C3 hold"), Synergy buildup (→ m-and-a.md synergy section)
**Kill conditions:** Customer concentration >40% in top 3 → walk away | Net synergies < acquisition premium → renegotiate or walk | Integration >24mo and culture incompatible → partnership instead | Competitive bidding above valuation range → disciplined walk
**Quality gates:** Deal thesis states WHY this target specifically; realization haircuts applied (60-80% cost, 40-60% revenue); every kill condition explicitly evaluated

### Pricing Strategy

**GT+:** "[Company] should restructure pricing from [current] to [target model], capturing $[X]M uplift ([Y]pp margin improvement) via [mechanism], phased over [timeframe], without material defection risk."
**GT−:** "[Company]'s pricing is near WTP ceiling. Further increases risk [X]% volume loss. Margin opportunity is in [cost/mix], not pricing."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Pricing Diagnostic | Value leakage quantified via PVM and pocket margin (→ pricing.md) |
| 2 | WTP Analysis | Customer willingness-to-pay validated via EVE or primary research (→ pricing.md) |
| 3 | Price Architecture | Tiers, bundles, fences designed; competitive response modeled |
| 4 | Implementation | Execution plan without customer defection risk |

**Key devices:** PVM decomposition (→ pricing.md), Pocket margin waterfall (→ pricing.md), EVE (→ pricing.md), Discount governance audit (→ pricing.md)
**Kill conditions:** WTP headroom <5% in core segments → no pricing action | Elasticity >−2.0 → volume loss exceeds price gain | Active price war confirmed → defend, don't optimize | NPS shows price as #1 detractor → fix value delivery first
**Quality gates:** $[X]M grounded in transaction data + WTP, not benchmarks alone; includes "without material defection risk"; phasing explicit (quick wins 0-3mo, architecture 3-9mo, full realization 9-18mo)

### Digital / AI Transformation

**GT+:** "[Company] should invest $[X]M over [timeframe] in [N] digital capabilities, prioritized by impact × feasibility, to capture $[Y]M annual value — starting with [top 2-3 use cases]."
**GT−:** "[Company] should not pursue enterprise transformation because [foundational blocker]. Instead, invest $[X]M in [prerequisite] first."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Digital Maturity Diagnostic | Gap to peers quantified; broken link in value chain identified |
| 2 | Use Case Prioritization | 40-60 candidates screened to 8-15 via impact × feasibility |
| 3 | Architecture & Capability | Build/buy/partner decided per capability; talent gap staffable |
| 4 | Transformation Roadmap | Phase 1 self-funds ≥50% of Phase 2 |

**Key devices:** AI Impact-Feasibility Matrix (→ BCG catalog.md), Digital Value Chain (6 stages: Capture → Integrate → Analyze → Automate → Channel → Learn), Three Horizons sequencing
**Kill conditions:** Data infrastructure absent (stages 1-2 score <2.0) → foundation-first | All scenarios below hurdle rate → defensive use cases only | >60% of BU leaders resist, sponsor lacks authority → org redesign first
**Quality gates:** ≥25 scored use cases with clear quadrant separation; Phase 1 generates measurable value within 6 months; talent plan within 15% of available pool

### Org Restructuring

**GT+:** "[Company] should restructure from [current model] to [target model], reducing [X] layers, achieving $[Y]M savings and [Z]% decision speed improvement, while preserving [critical capability]."
**GT−:** "[Company]'s decline is not structural — [root cause] is the constraint. Restructuring would destroy [capability]. Instead, [alternative]."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Organizational Diagnostic | ≥3 independent signals confirm structural misalignment |
| 2 | Target Model Design | Every option tested against capability preservation matrix |
| 3 | Transition Architecture | Transition cost fully loaded; phasing realistic |
| 4 | People & Change | Stakeholder engagement plan covers all high-influence individuals |

**Key devices:** RAPID Decision Maps (→ Bain process.md Loop 3), Span-of-control analysis, Decision audit (10-15 decisions, cycle time), SARS capability-preservation test (MANDATORY: map end-to-end processes that work well → preserve list)
**Kill conditions:** Root cause is leadership/strategy/culture, not structure → different intervention | Capability preservation test fails → hybrid model | Key talent attrition >20% projected → retention-first | CEO using restructuring to remove opponents → de-scope to performance management
**Quality gates:** [critical capability] MANDATORY in GT — must name what new model could destroy; negative variant prepared even if not presented; spans/layers benchmarked against ≥5 comparable orgs; RAPID maps show before AND after

### Commercial Excellence

**GT+:** "[Company] can increase commercial productivity by [X]% through [N] interventions across [scope], generating $[Y]M incremental revenue and improving [efficiency metric] by [Z]%."
**GT−:** "[Company]'s underperformance reflects [root cause: product-market fit gap], not execution failure. Commercial optimization yields <[X]%. Priority is [alternative]."

| # | Pillar | Must-Be-True |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Commercial Diagnostic | Customer economics decomposition identifies which funnel stage leaks value |
| 2 | Channel & Segment Optimization | Segment priorities survive sensitivity on top 2 weighting factors |
| 3 | Capability Building | Gaps defined as specific measurable deficiencies with benchmarks |
| 4 | Performance System | KPI architecture shows causal chain (leading → lagging) |

**Key devices:** Customer Economics Decomposition: Acquire → Activate → Retain → Monetize → Refer (each stage has conversion rate + cost); Segment attractiveness × capability fit matrix; Channel cost-to-serve variance; Win/loss attribution
**Kill conditions:** >50% of losses from product gaps → product intervention | Top-2 players >60% share with network effects → strategic options | >40% complaints from channel conflict → resolve conflict first | Rep performance variance tight (<1.5×) → demand generation, not sales execution
**Quality gates:** Customer economics covers >90% of value with data-sourced rates; benchmark against named peer set; channel ROI uses fully-loaded cost; quick wins achievable within 90 days
