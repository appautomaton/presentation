# Pricing Strategy — Skeleton

## Governing Thought

**Affirmative:**
```
"[Company] should restructure pricing from [current model] to [target model],
 capturing $[X]M in annual revenue uplift ([Y]% price realization improvement)
 by [mechanism], with implementation phased over [timeframe]."
```

**Negative:**
```
"[Company]'s pricing is not the binding constraint — [root cause: product/channel/cost]
 limits willingness-to-pay. Pricing optimization yields <[X]% uplift;
 priority investment is [alternative]."
```

## Pillar Architecture

| # | Pillar | Sub-Question | Transition Out |
|---|--------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Pricing Diagnostic** | Where is price leakage and how big is it? | Pivot → "leakage is $[X]M, addressable" |
| 2 | **Willingness-to-Pay** | What will customers actually pay? | Narrowing → "data supports [X]% increase in [segments]" |
| 3 | **Price Architecture** | What pricing model/structure? | Escalation → "architecture defined, execution is the risk" |
| 4 | **Implementation** | How to roll out without disruption? | Closes to recommendation |

## Kill Conditions

| # | Trigger | Pivot To |
|---|---------|---------|
| KC-1 | WTP analysis shows no headroom (customers at value ceiling) | Cost reduction or product differentiation |
| KC-2 | Competitive dynamics prevent unilateral price moves | Coordinated industry repricing or value-based differentiation |
| KC-3 | Sales force actively undermines pricing (>40% off-policy discounting) | Incentive redesign before pricing architecture |
| KC-4 | Price increases trigger regulatory scrutiny in this sector | Value-based pricing with compliance framework |

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Price leakage decomposed to actionable level (by segment, channel, deal size)
- [ ] WTP validated with real customer data (not just conjoint or internal estimates)
- [ ] Elasticity estimates include confidence intervals
- [ ] Architecture tested against ≥3 pricing models (value-based, competitive, cost-plus)
- [ ] Rollout plan includes sales enablement and customer communication
- [ ] P&L impact modeled with realization haircut (typically 60-80% of theoretical)
- [ ] Helicopter test: slides 2, 4, 7, 13, 18 tell the complete pricing story
