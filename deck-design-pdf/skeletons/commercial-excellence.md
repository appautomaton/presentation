# Commercial Excellence — Skeleton

## Governing Thought

**Affirmative:**
```
"[Company] can increase commercial productivity by [X]% through [N] interventions
 across [channels/segments], generating $[Y]M in incremental revenue
 and improving [efficiency metric] by [Z]%."
```

**Negative:**
```
"[Company]'s commercial underperformance reflects [root cause: product-market fit gap],
 not execution failure. Commercial optimization yields <[X]%;
 priority investment is [alternative]."
```

## Pillar Architecture

| # | Pillar | Sub-Question | Analytical Core | Transition Out |
|---|--------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Commercial Diagnostic** | Where is performance lagging and why? | Customer economics: Acquire × Activate × Retain × Monetize × Refer | Pivot → "opportunity is in [specific area]" |
| 2 | **Channel & Segment Optimization** | Which channels/segments to prioritize? | Segment attractiveness × capability fit; channel ROI | Narrowing → "of N segments, M warrant investment" |
| 3 | **Capability Building** | What commercial capabilities to build? | Gap assessment vs. best-in-class; sales force effectiveness | Escalation → "binding constraint is [capability]" |
| 4 | **Performance System** | How to measure, incentivize, sustain? | KPI cascade, incentive alignment, governance cadence | Closes to recommendation |

**Customer Economics Decomposition:** Acquire (CAC, conversion) → Activate (onboarding, first value) → Retain (churn, renewal) → Monetize (expansion, cross-sell) → Refer (NPS, referral rate). Most commercial problems trace to 1-2 of these stages.

## Kill Conditions

| # | Trigger | Pivot To |
|---|---------|---------|
| KC-1 | >50% of losses from product gaps, confirmed by customer interviews | Product-market fit intervention |
| KC-2 | Top-2 players >60% share with network effects | Strategic options (consolidate, niche, exit) |
| KC-3 | >40% of complaints from channel conflict | Channel conflict resolution before optimization |
| KC-4 | Rep performance variance is tight (<1.5×) — not a sales execution problem | Demand generation / marketing pivot |
| KC-5 | CRM data unreliable or absent | Data foundation before diagnostic |

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Customer economics decomposition covers >90% of value with data-sourced rates
- [ ] Benchmark gap against named peer set (n=, period, metric definition specified)
- [ ] Segment priorities survive sensitivity on top 2 weighting factors
- [ ] Channel ROI uses fully-loaded cost (including overhead allocation)
- [ ] Capability gaps defined as specific measurable deficiencies with benchmarks
- [ ] KPI architecture shows causal chain (leading → lagging)
- [ ] Quick wins achievable within 90 days; capability plan staffed and budgeted
- [ ] Helicopter test: CRO reads titles slides 2-20, gets full strategy in 2 minutes
